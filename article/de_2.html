Wolff call for fining teams who submit “long shot” protests

GRANDPRIX247.comIn-Depth & Independent Formula 1 News All The TimeMercedes boss Toto Wolff, speaking to the media at the Austrian Grand Prix in Spielberg on Friday, said Formula 1 teams should be fined if they fail in ‘long shot’ protests against rivals. The Austrian told reporters at his home grand prix that the governing FIA was already looking into the possibility of such a measure. Rivals Red Bull have protested twice in the space of five races, and without success, against Mercedes driver George Russell.
